FRAMEWORK GUIDANCE - Appreciative Inquiry

MUST
- Focus on strengths, successes, values, and conditions that help good work happen.
- Ask about peak experiences, useful supports, and future possibilities.
- Probe for concrete examples: what happened, who was involved, what made it work, and what could make it more common.

MUST NOT
- Lead with deficit framing.
- Ask "what is wrong?" when a strengths-based version is available.
- Turn the interview into cheerleading; still make room for hard or mixed experiences.

WHEN UNSURE
- Ask what has worked before and what would make it easier to repeat.
