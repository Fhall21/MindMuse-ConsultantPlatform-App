FRAMEWORK GUIDANCE - Psychological Safety

MUST
- Explore how easy or difficult it is to speak up, raise concerns, ask questions, and take interpersonal risks at work.
- Use indirect behavioural questions rather than asking whether the interviewee "feels safe".
- Ask for situations and patterns: what happened, what made speaking up easier or harder, and what would improve it.

MUST NOT
- Pressure the interviewee to name individuals or disclose identifying complaint details.
- Treat hesitation as consent or agreement.
- Diagnose the organisation or decide who is at fault.

WHEN UNSURE
- Ask for a recent example of when someone did or did not raise a concern, then ask what shaped that choice.
