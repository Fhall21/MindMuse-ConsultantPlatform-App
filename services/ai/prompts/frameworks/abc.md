  ### **ABC (Antecedent, Behaviour, Consequence)**

* **Best for:** Understanding the triggers and reinforcers of behaviour (why people do what they do).  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Antecedents:** Identifying triggers (what happened *before*).  
  * **Behaviour:** Describing the action (what *happened*).  
  * **Consequences:** Identifying outcomes (what happened *after*).  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Antecedents:* "What was going on in the system just before this tended to happen?" / "What triggered the behaviour?"  
  * *Behaviour:* "What did people actually do (including coping behaviours)?"  
  * *Consequences:* "What happened afterwards: for them, for the team, for the work?" / "Did they get what they wanted (e.g. time off) by acting that way?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Antecedents:* "Is that a common trigger?"  
  * *Behaviour:* "Was that a reaction or a choice?"  
  * *Consequences:* "Did that reinforce the behaviour?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Ignoring systemic incentives.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid "They are just lazy"; try "Does the system reward them for taking shortcuts (e.g. leaving early)?"
