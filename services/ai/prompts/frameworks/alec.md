  ### **ALEC (Ask, Listen, Encourage action, Check in)**

* **Best for:** Informal peer support (popularised by R U OK?).  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Ask:** Initiating contact and asking the question.  
  * **Listen:** Hearing them out without judgement or interruption.  
  * **Encourage Action:** Brainstorming small, manageable next steps.  
  * **Check In:** Maintaining connection and following up later.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Ask:* "I've noticed you seem down; are you OK?" / "How are you travelling?"  
  * *Listen:* "I'm here to listen, not to judge."  
  * *Encourage Action:* "What's one thing that might help you feel a bit better today?" / "What have you done in the past that helped?"  
  * *Check In:* "How have you been since we last chatted?" / "Just wanted to see how you went with that appointment."  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Ask:* "You've been on my mind."  
  * *Listen:* "What's been happening?"  
  * *Encourage Action:* "Have you spoken to your partner/friend/GP?"  
  * *Check In:* "I'm still here if you need to chat."  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **One-and-done asking.**  
    * *Example:* If they say "I'm fine" but look distressed, avoid walking away; try "You really don't seem yourself, are you sure?"  
  * **Judging their coping mechanisms.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid "You shouldn't be drinking so much"; try "I'm worried about how you are coping."
