### **BRIDGE**

* **Best for:** Leaders to create psychological safety in day to day interactions.  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Be approachable**: Ensure your physical and communication style invites people to come to you with problems.  
  * **Respond constructively**: Handle initial feedback or concerns with genuine curiosity and without defensiveness.  
  * **Invite participation:** Actively and equally seeking input from all.  
  * **Demonstrate fallibility:** Model that mistakes and uncertainty are acceptable.  
  * **Generate inquiry:** Ask questions that show genuine curiosity and seek to understand the context, not questions that lead to a judgment.  
  * **Establish norms:** Create explicit, agreed-upon rules for teamwork and conflict.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Be approachable:* "I have 15 minutes right now if you need to chat about anything." / (Non-verbal) Keep my door open and maintain an open posture.  
  * *Respond constructively:* "Thank you for bringing that up; help me understand what was happening at the time." / "I appreciate your honesty."  
  * *Invite participation:* "I haven't heard from \[Quiet Person\], what's your take?" / "Before we move on, what holes do you see in this idea?"  
  * *Demonstrate fallibility:* "I made an incorrect assumption on that last project; here's the impact." / "To be honest, I'm not 100% sure how to handle this."  
  * *Generate inquiry:* "Tell me more about what you mean by 'clumsy process'?" / "What part of the system made that mistake easy to make?"  
  * *Establish norms:* "Let's agree that in this team, we talk to the person directly first." / "Our norm is 'no email threads after 7pm'."  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Be approachable:* "Anything about my schedule making me harder to reach?"  
  * *Respond constructively:* "What is the next step you recommend?"  
  * *Invite participation:* "Is there a better way for me to ask for your input?"  
  * *Demonstrate fallibility:* "What did you learn from my mistake?"  
  * *Generate inquiry:* "Why did that decision make sense to you in the moment?"  
  * *Establish norms:* "What should the consequence be if we violate that norm?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Defensiveness.** A negative reaction to honest feedback will immediately shut down future communication (violates Respond constructively).  
    * *Example:* Avoid "That's not fair; I'm always available"; try "It seems I'm harder to reach than I thought, what signals would help show I'm open?"  
  * **Posing questions as accusations.** The goal is learning, not a veiled investigation (violates Generate inquiry).  
    * *Example:* Avoid "Why did you ignore the procedure?"; try "Walk me through the steps you took and why you chose that path."
