### **CARE (Check in, Actively listen, Reassure, Encourage help)**

* **Best for:** Early intervention check-ins when you notice someone might be struggling but aren't sure.  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Check in:** Starting the conversation gently and observationally.  
  * **Actively listen:** Giving full attention without distractions or judgment.  
  * **Reassure:** Validating feelings and normalising the experience.  
  * **Encourage help:** Signposting professional support options.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Check in:* "Are you OK? How's life lately?" / "I've noticed you've been quiet; is everything alright?"  
  * *Actively listen:* "I see... take your time." (Use silence/nodding)  
  * *Reassure:* "You're not a burden; I'm here to listen." / "It's okay to not be okay."  
  * *Encourage help:* "Have you thought about chatting to the EAP?" / "Do you have a GP you trust?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Check in:* "You don't seem like yourself."  
  * *Actively listen:* "It sounds like you are carrying a lot right now."  
  * *Reassure:* "You are not alone in feeling this way."  
  * *Encourage help:* "Can I help you make that call?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Rushing to fix.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid "You should just take a holiday"; try "What do you think would help right now?"  
  * **Toxic positivity.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid "Cheer up, it could be worse"; try "It sounds like things are really tough for you."
