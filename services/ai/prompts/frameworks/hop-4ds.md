### **HOP 4Ds (Dangerous, Difficult, Dumb, Different)**

* **Best for:** Running quick, non-threatening scans of a team's daily reality to find where "work-as-done" is clashing with "work-as-imagined", uncovering system issues without blaming individuals.  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Dangerous:** Identifying parts of work that feel unsafe, high-stakes, or potentially damaging (mentally or physically).  
  * **Difficult:** Highlighting tasks that are inherently hard, draining, clumsy, or stressful to execute due to poor design or tools.  
  * **Dumb:** Normalising feedback on rules, forms, or processes that don't make sense or add value to the people doing the work.  
  * **Different:** Contrasting official procedures (work-as-imagined) with actual practice (work-as-done).  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Dangerous:* "Are there any tasks where you feel like you're walking on eggshells?" / "What parts of the day feel the most unsafe or high-pressure?"  
  * *Difficult:* "What’s the hardest thing you have to do in a typical week?" / "Which task makes you groan when you see it on your list?"  
  * *Dumb:* "If you could delete one form or process tomorrow, what would it be?" / "What are we doing that feels like 'tick-the-box' work?"  
  * *Different:* "Where do you have to ignore the official procedure just to get the job done?" / "What’s the 'real' way you handle this task versus what the manual says?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Dangerous:* "Is that a physical risk, or does it just drain you mentally?"  
  * *Difficult:* "Is it difficult because it's complex, or because the tools make it hard?"  
  * *Dumb:* "Does that rule actually help us, or does it just slow you down?"  
  * *Different:* "Why does the official way not work in practice?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Blaming individuals for "dumb" rules or "different" methods.** Focus entirely on the system logic.  
    * *Example:* Instead of "Why do you do it the wrong way?", try "Why does it make sense to do it that way in this situation?"  
  * **Undermining authority or safety protocols.** Ensure psychological safety is established before asking about "Dumb" things so it isn't seen as insubordination.  
    * *Example:* Avoid asking "Who made up this stupid rule?" in front of management; instead ask "Does this rule achieve the intended safety outcome in practice?"
