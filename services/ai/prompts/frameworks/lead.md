  ### **LEAD (Listen, Example, Awareness, Discover)**

* **Best for:** Values-based feedback for behavioural correction; helps maintain the relationship while correcting behaviour.  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Listen:** Reaffirming values and asking for their view.  
  * **Example:** Sharing your own fallibility (humility) to build connection.  
  * **Awareness:** Explaining the lesson learned and the impact.  
  * **Discover:** Asking them to solve it.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Listen:* "One of our values is Respect... help me understand what happened from your view."  
  * *Example:* "I recall early in my career I lost my temper too..." / "I've struggled with this myself."  
  * *Awareness:* "I learned that an outburst can hurt team morale." / "I realised it damaged my reputation."  
  * *Discover:* "Going forward, how do you think you can handle frustration while upholding respect?" / "What could you do differently next time?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Listen:* "What was your intention there?"  
  * *Example:* "It's a hard lesson to learn."  
  * *Awareness:* "It took me a while to rebuild that trust."  
  * *Discover:* "How can I support you with that?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Lecturing.** The person must participate.  
    * *Example:* Avoid "You need to do X, Y, Z"; try "What do you think is the best way to fix this?"  
  * **Superiority.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid "I would never do that"; try "I know how hard it is to stay calm."
