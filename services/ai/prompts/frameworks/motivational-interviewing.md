  ### **Motivational Interviewing (OARS & Change Talk)**

* **Best for:** A conversational style to use across all frameworks, especially when someone is ambivalent about change.  
* **Steps (OARS):**  
  * **Open questions:** Encouraging storytelling rather than Yes/No answers.  
  * **Affirmations:** Building confidence and rapport.  
  * **Reflections:** Showing understanding and mirroring feelings.  
  * **Summaries:** Recapping and checking understanding.  
  * **Strategy:** Listening for "Change talk" (desire to change) vs "Sustain talk" (reasons to stay the same).  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Open questions:* "Tell me about a recent week that really drained you."  
  * *Affirmations:* "You've put a lot of effort into coping with very tricky demands." / "I appreciate your honesty."  
  * *Reflections:* "It sounds like you feel torn between wanting to speak up and worrying it might backfire."  
  * *Summaries:* "Let me check I've got this: you are feeling X because of Y..."  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Open questions:* "Tell me more about that."  
  * *Affirmations:* "That took courage."  
  * *Reflections:* "You seem really frustrated by that."  
  * *Summaries:* "Did I miss anything?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **The "Righting Reflex".** Trying to fix or persuade creates resistance.  
    * *Example:* Avoid "But you have to change or you'll get fired"; try "What do you see as the risks of staying as you are?"
