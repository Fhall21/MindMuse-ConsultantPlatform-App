  ### **"Notice-Open-Listen-Support" (Empathic Leadership)**

* **Best for:** Managers addressing performance dips or behavioural changes sensitively.  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Notice:** Observing changes privately (withdrawal, errors, mood changes).  
  * **Open:** Raising the topic gently using "I" statements and facts.  
  * **Listen:** Creating space and validating the experience.  
  * **Support:** Planning practical next steps and adjustments together.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Open:* "I've noticed you've been arriving late recently, and I just wanted to check if you need support?" / "You seem a bit distracted lately, is everything ok?"  
  * *Listen:* "I'm not here to judge; I just want to understand what's happening."  
  * *Support:* "What can we adjust in your workload to help you through this?" / "Can we move some deadlines?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Open:* "Is there anything work-related contributing to this?"  
  * *Listen:* "That sounds really tough."  
  * *Support:* "Let's catch up on Friday to see how you are going."  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Ambushing.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid bringing this up in a team meeting; try "Can we grab a quiet room for 10 minutes?"  
  * **Making it disciplinary.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid "This is your first warning"; try "I want to help you get back on track."
