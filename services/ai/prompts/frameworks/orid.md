  ### **ORID (Objective, Reflective, Interpretive, Decisional)**

* **Best for:** Structured facilitation in focus groups to move from facts to action without getting stuck in complaining.  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Objective:** Gathering facts and observations (The "What").  
  * **Reflective:** Gathering feelings, reactions, and emotional impacts (The "Gut").  
  * **Interpretive:** Making meaning and analysing implications (The "So What").  
  * **Decisional:** Planning concrete actions and next steps (The "Now What").  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Objective:* "What actually happens day-to-day? What tasks, tools, hours?" / "What are the facts of the situation?"  
  * *Reflective:* "What parts of your job leave you tired, frustrated, or worried?" / "How did that make you feel?"  
  * *Interpretive:* "What do these experiences tell us about how the work is designed?" / "What is the root cause here?"  
  * *Decisional:* "What are the 1–2 most important things we should change first?" / "What are we going to do about it?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Objective:* "What did you actually see or hear?"  
  * *Reflective:* "What was your immediate reaction?"  
  * *Interpretive:* "What does this mean for our performance?"  
  * *Decisional:* "Who will do what by when?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Skipping the "Reflective" step.** Emotions must be aired before solutions.  
    * *Example:* Avoid jumping from "The printer is broken" to "Buy a new printer"; try asking "How does the broken printer impact your stress levels?" first.  
  * **Getting stuck in "Interpretive" analysis paralysis.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid debating the theory for hours; try "Given what we know, what is one small step we can take?"
