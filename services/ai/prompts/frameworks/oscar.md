  ### **OSCAR (Outcome, Situation, Choices, Actions, Review)**

* **Best for:** Situational coaching, especially for recurring problems (e.g. chronic overtime or conflict).  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Outcome:** Desired result.  
  * **Situation:** Current context and triggers.  
  * **Choices:** Available paths and consequences.  
  * **Actions:** Specific steps.  
  * **Review:** Evaluation.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Outcome:* "What do you want to achieve here?" / "How do you want this to end?"  
  * *Situation:* "What is triggering this situation repeatedly?" / "Describe the current state."  
  * *Choices:* "What choices do you have when that happens?" / "What are the pros and cons of that?"  
  * *Actions:* "What will you do next time the trigger occurs?"  
  * *Review:* "How will we know if it worked?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Outcome:* "What does success look like?"  
  * *Situation:* "When does this usually happen?"  
  * *Choices:* "What is the risk of doing nothing?"  
  * *Actions:* "When will you start?"  
  * *Review:* "Let's review in a week."  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Staying in "Situation".** Move to action.  
    * *Example:* Avoid letting them vent for 40 minutes about the problem; try "We understand the problem well now, let's look at Choices."
