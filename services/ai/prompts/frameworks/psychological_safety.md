# Psychological Safety Ladder

* **Best for:** Teams with low trust or fear of speaking up; helps diagnose where the team is stuck.
* **Steps:**
  * **Leader Vulnerability:** The leader admits mistakes or uncertainty first to signal safety (start small if needed).
  * **Safe Challenge Zones:** Explicitly inviting dissent and questioning of plans.
  * **Mistake Normalisation:** Framing errors as learning opportunities rather than failures.
  * **Feedback Loops:** Structuring regular, multidirectional feedback.
  * **Performance through Trust:** Linking safety to better business outcomes (speed, innovation).
* **Sentence Starters:**
  * *Leader Vulnerability:* "I messed up on this projection; here is what I learned." / "I'm actually not sure of the answer, what do you think?"
  * *Safe Challenge Zones:* "I want everyone to poke holes in this plan; what are we missing?" / "Who sees a reason why this won't work?"
  * *Mistake Normalisation:* "Let's share our 'Friday Fails' and what we learned." / "Who has a 'learning moment' to share?"
  * *Feedback Loops:* "What should we start, stop, and continue doing as a team?"
  * *Performance through Trust:* "Our faster problem-solving is a result of everyone speaking up."
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**
  * *Leader Vulnerability:* "Has anyone else felt unsure lately?"
  * *Safe Challenge Zones:* "Don't hold back; I need your honest eyes on this."
  * *Mistake Normalisation:* "How can we ensure that doesn't happen again?"
  * *Feedback Loops:* "Are we actually acting on this feedback?"
  * *Performance through Trust:* "Can we see the difference in our results?"
* **Avoid:**
  * **Demanding trust.** You have to model it.
    * *Example:* Avoid "Why won't you tell me the truth?"; try "I clearly haven't made it safe enough to speak up—what can I do differently?"
  * **Punishing the messenger.**
    * *Example:* Avoid getting defensive when challenged; try "Thank you for pointing that out, I missed that risk."
