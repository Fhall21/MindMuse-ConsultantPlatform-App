  ### **SMART Work Design Framework** {#smart-work-design-framework}

* **Best for:** Proactively designing jobs to be healthier, more engaging, and motivating. It targets poor work design rather than focusing solely on individual coping.   
* **Steps:** The framework uses five key themes for creating quality work:  
  * **S**timulating: Ensuring the job involves skill variety, task variety, and problem-solving opportunities.  
  * **M**astery: Providing clarity on roles, ensuring regular feedback, and allowing for task identity (seeing a task from beginning to end).  
  * **A**gency: Giving employees autonomy and control over their work schedule, methods, and decision-making. (Note: Autonomy is sometimes referred to as Agency.)  
  * **R**elational: Building in opportunities for support, social contact, and connection, as well as linking work to a meaningful purpose.  
  * **T**olerable Demands: Keeping job demands (eg. workload, time pressure, and emotional demands) manageable and reasonable.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Stimulating:* "What opportunities are there to use a variety of your skills in this role?" / "How often does your job require creative problem-solving?"  
  * *Mastery:* "Do you receive clear feedback on your performance, and how often?" / "Do you feel like you complete a whole piece of work, or just fragments?"  
  * *Agency:* "How much control do you have over your daily schedule or the methods you use?" / "Where could we give the team more decision-making authority?"  
  * *Relational:* "What structures can we put in place to enhance teamwork and support?" / "How does this job connect to our overall organizational purpose?"  
  * *Tolerable Demands:* "On a typical day, are your job demands manageable, or are you consistently putting in excessive overtime?" / "Are the expectations for performance reasonable and consistent?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Stimulating:* "Would increasing task variety make the work more interesting?"  
  * *Mastery:* "What information is missing for you to have complete role clarity?"  
  * *Agency:* "Would more autonomy here increase or decrease your stress?"  
  * *Relational:* "Is the current level of social contact enough to feel connected to the team?"  
  * *Tolerable Demands:* "If we cannot reduce the demand, what support do you need?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Focusing on 'fixing the person'** (e.g., resilience training) instead of fixing the job design, which is the root cause of stress.  
    * *Example:* Avoid "You need to manage your time better"; try "We need to reconfigure the role tasks to reduce unmanageable demands."  
  * **Ignoring the systemic nature** of work design problems. The model should be implemented company-wide or team-by-team.  
    * *Example:* Avoid "This is just how the job is".
