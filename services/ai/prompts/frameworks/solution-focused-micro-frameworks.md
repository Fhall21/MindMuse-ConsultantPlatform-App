  ### **Solution-Focused Micro-frameworks**

* **Best for:** Brief, positive interventions to shift mindset from "stuck" to "possible".  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Preferred future:** Visualising success.  
  * **Exceptions:** Finding what works (times when the problem isn't there).  
  * **Scaling:** Measuring progress numerically.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Preferred future:* "If there were no constraints, what would your ideal day look like?" / "If you woke up tomorrow and the problem was gone, what would you notice?"  
  * *Exceptions:* "When is this problem less intense? What's different on those days?" / "Tell me about a time you handled this well."  
  * *Scaling:* "On a 0–10 scale of coping, where are you now? What would move you from a 4 to a 5?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Preferred future:* "What would you be doing differently?"  
  * *Exceptions:* "How did you make that happen?"  
  * *Scaling:* "What is stopping it from being a 3?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Problem Saturation.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid asking "Why is this happening?" repeatedly; try "When is this *not* happening?"  
  * **The "Miracle Question" without rapport.** It can feel dismissive.  
    * *Example:* Avoid asking them to imagine a perfect world if they are in crisis; stick to "Scaling" (small steps).
