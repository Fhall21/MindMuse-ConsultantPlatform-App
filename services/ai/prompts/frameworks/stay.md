### **STAY (Stay present, Talk gently, Acknowledge pain, Yield judgement)**

* **Best for:** Crisis conversations (e.g. panic attacks, acute distress, suicidal ideation).  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Stay present:** Grounding yourself and signalling safety; remaining physically present.  
  * **Talk gently:** Using a soft tone, open questions, and silence.  
  * **Acknowledge pain:** Validating their experience and emotion without minimising.  
  * **Yield judgement:** Suspending all criticism, fixing, and shock.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Stay present:* "I'm here with you; I'm not going anywhere."  
  * *Talk gently:* "Can you tell me what's brought you to feel this way?" / "Take your time, there is no rush."  
  * *Acknowledge pain:* "That sounds incredibly painful; I'm sorry you are carrying this." / "It makes sense that you feel overwhelmed."  
  * *Yield judgement:* (Avoid saying "at least" or "it will be fine" \- say nothing, just listen).  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Stay present:* "I've got time."  
  * *Talk gently:* (Silence)  
  * *Acknowledge pain:* "That is a heavy load."  
  * *Yield judgement:* "I hear you."  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Panicking.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid "Oh my god, what do we do?"; try taking a deep breath and staying calm.  
  * **Ignoring your own safety.**  
    * *Example:* If the person is aggressive or unsafe, call for help immediately; do not "Stay present" if you are in danger.
