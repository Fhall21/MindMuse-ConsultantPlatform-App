  ### **Team Support Loops**

* **Best for:** Spreading support responsibility across the team (peer-to-peer) so it doesn't all fall on the manager.  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Buddy systems:** Pairing colleagues for mutual support and check-ins.  
  * **Mental Health Champions:** Training volunteers to be first points of contact.  
  * **Rotating wellbeing talks:** Sharing the responsibility for leading safety moments.  
  * **Cross-team networks:** Connecting people with similar lived experiences.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Buddy systems:* "Let's set up weekly coffee chats so everyone has a buddy." / "Who is your 'go-to' person this week?"  
  * *Mental Health Champions:* "Who would like to be trained as a go-to point for initial support?" / "We have champions available if you need a confidential chat."  
  * *Rotating wellbeing talks:* "Who wants to share a wellbeing tip at the next meeting?" / "Let's rotate who leads the check-in."  
  * *Cross-team networks:* "Let's connect the new parents across departments." / "Is there a group for remote workers?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Buddy systems:* "Are you finding those chats useful?"  
  * *Mental Health Champions:* "Do you feel supported in that role?"  
  * *Rotating wellbeing talks:* "What's one thing you do to unwind?"  
  * *Cross-team networks:* "How can we support that group?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Voluntelling.** Participation must be voluntary.  
    * *Example:* Avoid "You are the buddy for X"; try "Who would be open to being a buddy for X?"  
  * **Treating champions as counsellors.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid sending complex crisis cases to a peer champion; try "The champion is there to listen and refer you to the EAP."
