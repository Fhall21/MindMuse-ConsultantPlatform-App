  ### **Trauma-Informed Leadership Principles**

* **Best for:** Creating a safe environment, especially for diverse teams or those with past trauma (though good for everyone).  
* **Steps:**  
  * **Safety:** Prioritising physical and psychological safety above speed.  
  * **Trustworthiness & Transparency:** Communicating openly and honestly about decisions.  
  * **Collaboration:** Doing *with* employees, not *to* them.  
  * **Empowerment & Choice:** Giving control and agency back to the individual.  
  * **Cultural Sensitivity:** Recognising and respecting diverse backgrounds and experiences.  
* **Sentence Starters:**  
  * *Safety:* "What do we need to agree on so we can speak openly today?" / "What do you need to feel comfortable in this meeting?"  
  * *Trustworthiness & Transparency:* "I want to explain exactly why this decision was made." / "Here is what I know, and here is what I can't share right now."  
  * *Collaboration:* "How would you like to be involved in this process?" / "Let's build this plan together."  
  * *Empowerment & Choice:* "You have the choice of how you want to approach this task." / "What works best for you?"  
  * *Cultural Sensitivity:* "How might this policy impact people from different backgrounds?" / "Is there a cultural perspective we are missing?"  
* **Sentence Follow-ups:**  
  * *Safety:* "Is there anything we should remove from the room?"  
  * *Trustworthiness & Transparency:* "Does that make sense?"  
  * *Collaboration:* "What part of this do you want to own?"  
  * *Empowerment & Choice:* "Do you need any resources to do it that way?"  
  * *Cultural Sensitivity:* "Have we consulted with the right groups?"  
* **Avoid:**  
  * **Assuming safety.** Just because you feel safe doesn't mean they do.  
    * *Example:* Avoid "Everyone feels fine here"; try "I know it can be hard to speak up in these meetings."  
  * **Over-promising transparency.**  
    * *Example:* Avoid "I will tell you everything" (if you can't); try "I will be as open as I can be within the legal limits."
